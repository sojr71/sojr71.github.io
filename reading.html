Current Read: Lies and Sorcery by Elsa Morante

  Lies and Sorcery (1948) is the latest in the line of the long book. Elsa Morante wrote it in Italy in the wake of the second world war. I'm currently about half of the way through the book, and so far the book is almost entirely about the minute details of the narrator's family going back three generations. It is an extremely character-study heavy book, so I'm missing some plot, but the book is incredibly well written.

Most Recent Finished Book:Infinite Jest by David Foster Wallace

 Infinite Jest (1996) was my most recent finished read, and it took my a good two months to get through it. It is as pretentious and convoluted as its reputation would suggest. When I mentioned "Long Book 2026" to people, IJ was the book that was suggested or asked about most frequently. It's kind of the quintessential literature-bro book. A lot of the time reading the book is spent flipping to te end notes for clarification, definitions, explanations and at one point the fictional filmography of one of the characters. It is pretty satisfying to work through, though, and when things click, they feel really satisfying.
