Books I'm Currently Reading

The Secret History by Donna Tartt


 The Secret History is my current read. At time of writing, I have read through approximately half of the novel so far, and I'm starting to get the hype. Before I started reading, I had heard again and again that the book was pretentious, and boy is that right. However, I was thinking House of Leaves or Infinite Jest style pretension, but it is different in what feels like an important distinction. The Secret History is about pretentious people who view the world through their very specific classics-inflected lens. It draws heavily upon allusions to the classics, but at no point is the reader required to stop and check self-indulgent foot- or endnotes. So while there is a pretentious air to the novel, it feels like a self conscious critique of the type of real world pretenses that distort our characters minds and actions.

 The book is set at a tiny, elite private college in Vermont, and in the prologue, the reader finds out from the first line that one of the main characters is to be murdered by the rest of the characters. The rest of the first half of the book describes the lead-up to the aforementioned murder. I will say that this book is making me awfully nostalgic for college (except for the murder and other unpleasantries parts). The prose is really clear and Tartt's use of imagery is really impactful, especially as the book speeds towards the murder. I will update this review once the second half of the book is read.
